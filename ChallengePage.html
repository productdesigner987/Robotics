Innovation & Ideas

Some of the hardest things about robotics are creativity and ideas. Even professionals have a hard time with this. It mostly depends on the year's topic and goal, but most innovative ideas are totally from yourself. You would think about how to solve the problem in your own creative way, whether it is far-fetched, or practical, every idea is acceptable. Robots are different from humans and can do things that many can't. Don't try to be original. Make up your own stuff. You should first take the pencil and paper before doing anything else.

Hardware & Software

Although many people struggle with innovative ideas, other have problems with hardware and software. Programming experience comes from practicing, and can only be mastered when working hard. It's easier to start out basic like things with programming blocks and then advance into the harder levels. Programmers take time to master this skill, yet many still have trouble figuring out what to code. Hardware, however, is harder for others, sometimes. It's difficult to imagine what you want to build and then put it into action without any instructions. Like software, it's best to start easy first with simple instructions to follow. In building, there can be many challenges you have to brave through, such as where to put a particular piece or how to turn it so the gears will turn with it. All of this, though, can be mastered through practice.
